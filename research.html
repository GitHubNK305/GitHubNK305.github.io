Research

Vision

Artificial intelligence (AI) assumes a crucial role in influencing the trajectory of the future, predominantly through the automation of repetitive tasks and the augmentation of human labor. The contemporary world is undergoing a profound digitization, characterized by an expanding network of sensors, computers, and intelligent devices, resulting in the generation of immense volumes of data. This data surpasses human capacity for processing, interpretation, and utilization. The escalating computational power has enabled machine learning to provide comprehensive solutions for intricate tasks, such as image recognition and natural language processing. Nevertheless, applying conventional methods designed for static data may yield unreliable outcomes in dynamic systems subject to temporal changes. My research is specifically dedicated to scrutinizing and formulating principle-based techniques tailored to dynamic environments, with the overarching goal of achieving trustworthy and secure machine learning.

The focal point of my research agenda is to augment the autonomy of machine systems by pushing the boundaries of machine learning algorithms and implementing them in real-world dynamic systems challenges. This entails not only the development of novel machine learning algorithms to enhance their performance but also the bridging of the divide between theoretical machine learning research and practical applications by addressing complex problems within this domain.

Research Topic One

Dynamic Modelling and Intelligent Control of Parafoil system

The precise and non-destructive recovery of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) will contribute to their recycling, maximizing cost reduction for launches, and ensuring landing area safety. This has significant practical significance and application value. For small and medium-sized fixed-wing UAVs, spacecraft, and launch components (rocket boosters, fairings, etc.) without landing gear, a reusable parafoil recovery system is adopted. By controlling the parafoil's trailing edge downward, adjusting the return trajectory, and performing wind-facing precision landing, precise and non-destructive recovery can be achieved. Therefore, the parafoil recovery system has broad application prospects in military, civilian, aerospace, and other fields.

With the support of the National Natural Science Foundation of China (62003175, 61973172) and the National Key Research and Development Program (2019YFC1510900), the applicant comprehensively researched and developed relevant theories and applications of parafoil non-destructive recovery. Aimed at achieving the modeling and precise return of parafoil systems in complex wind and rain environments, a combined approach of theoretical analysis, numerical simulation, and airdrop experiments was employed. In-depth research was conducted on parafoil system dynamics, return trajectory planning and tracking control, airdrop experiments, and other issues.
